Connections Manual

The team I'm on helps me feel included at work.
This question explores whether employees feel valued, respected, and included as part of their team. Positive feedback indicates a strong sense of belonging, while negative feedback may suggest underlying cultural issues or gaps in integration.
  • Promote team activities and events that encourage interaction and connection among team members.
  • Encourage managers to foster a culture of respect, inclusion, and recognition within the team.
  • Provide training on diversity, equity, and inclusion to help teams understand and appreciate different perspectives.
The team I'm on makes decisions quickly.
This question assesses how efficiently the team communicates and collaborates to make timely decisions. Positive feedback highlights a proactive and streamlined approach, while negative feedback indicates potential barriers in decision-making processes.
  • Streamline communication by ensuring clear and concise exchanges during discussions.
  • Set clear guidelines for decision-making authority to avoid delays.
  • Provide tools or frameworks for evaluating options and making informed decisions faster.
I can see how my work contributes to innovation in my organization.
This question evaluates whether employees understand how their roles align with and contribute to the organization's innovative efforts. Positive feedback indicates strong alignment, while negative feedback suggests gaps in communication or goal-setting.
  • Encourage regular communication from leadership on the organization's innovation goals and progress.
  • Provide opportunities for employees to contribute ideas and participate in innovative projects.
  • Recognize and celebrate individual contributions to innovation efforts.
My team has well-defined goals.
This question examines whether team members have a clear understanding of their objectives and how they align with broader organizational goals. Positive feedback reflects effective goal-setting, while negative feedback suggests areas for improvement in communication and prioritization.
  • Regularly review and update team goals to ensure they remain relevant and aligned with organizational objectives.
  • Break down goals into specific, actionable tasks, and communicate them clearly to all team members.
  • Track progress through regular check-ins and adjust goals as needed.
I can achieve my career goals at Amazon.
This question evaluates whether employees feel that their current roles and the organization's support align with their career aspirations. Positive feedback reflects strong career development opportunities, while negative feedback highlights gaps in resources or alignment.
  • Provide access to training programs and resources for skill development and career growth.
  • Encourage managers to have regular career development discussions with their team members.
  • Offer mentorship programs and networking opportunities to support career advancement.
